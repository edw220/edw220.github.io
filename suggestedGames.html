Below I have selected a handful of games I believe to be varying levels of challenge to learn. I've ordered them from easiest to most difficult, and included examples of my runs so you may get an idea of how challenging the runs are or what you might expect.

Astalon: Tears of the Earth

Astalon is a 2D action platformer focused on swapping between characters to solve puzzles or defeat enemies. It is a metroidvania, and while it has a few quirks in how it plays, I found the overall experience to be enjoyable. There are a couple categories on the speedrun page including Any%, 100%, or even separate modes like Black Knight. Below you'll find a recording of my latest run of Black Knight mode. I've chosen this as the most approachable run due to it's length of about 15-20 minutes, and the general simplicity of movement. This game can be played on Nintendo Switch, PC, PlayStation 4, Xbox One, and a first playthrough will take you somewhere between 15-20 hours to beat.

The Messenger

The Messenger is a 2D action platformer with strong level design that lends itself well to speedrunning. There are a number of pathways through most screens with varying skill required to execute. The story has a few twists, so I won't say too much about it, but if you enjoyed retro classics like Ninja Gaiden this is a must play. The primary category for The Messenger is called "8-Bit No Out of Bounds" which runs approximately 30-60 minutes depending on skill level. Below you'll find a recording of my latest run of 8-Bit No OOB. This speedrun has the benefit of being approachable while also having a lot of room for optimization. This is an excellent starting point for those who are already familiar with platforming basics and really want to break into speedrunning in general. This game can be played on Nintendo Switch, PC, PlayStation 4, Xbox One, and a first playthrough will take you somewhere between 12-25 hours to beat.

Metroid: Dread

Metroid: Dread is a 3D action platformer that takes place on a 2D plane. The fact that this game is at its core a 3D game lends itself to some of the more complex tricks that it offers. Metroid: Dread has a high barrier for entry for skill, and is not for those who are easily dissuaded by difficulty. Because of all of that, mastery of Dread ends up feeling intensely satisfying. Any% No Major Glitches (NMG) is the category I'd recommend to those just starting out with this game. Below you'll find my latest recorded run of Metroid: Dread Any% NMG. This game is only available on Nintendo Switch. If you are interested in speedrunning it, I would recommend purchasing a digital version and storing it on the storage local to the Switch. The reason is because utilizing both the digital version and local storage will save you literal minutes over the course of an Any% run. A first playthrough of Metroid: Dread will take you somewhere between 20-40 hours to beat.
